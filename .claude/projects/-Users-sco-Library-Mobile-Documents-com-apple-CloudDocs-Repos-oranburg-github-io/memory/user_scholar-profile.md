Seth C. Oranburg is a law professor (currently UNH Franklin Pierce School of Law, 2024-2026) whose scholarship sits at the intersection of technological disruption, institutional trust, and legal design. He has 28+ published works spanning 2015-2026.

**Research areas (in order of current emphasis):**
1. Digital assets, blockchain, and cryptocurrency regulation (GENIUS Act, CLARITY Act, DeFi)
2. Startup finance, securities law, and crowdfunding (JOBS Act, Series A gap, equity crowdfunding)
3. Corporate governance, ESG, and institutional design
4. Labor law, gig economy, and technology
5. Legal education and pedagogy
6. Intellectual property and trade secrets

**Other affiliations:**
- Director, Program on Organizations, Business and Markets, NYU Law Classical Liberal Institute
- Chair, Law Faculty Section, Academic Engagement Network
- Fellow, Faculty of Law, Haifa Center for Law and Technology

**Background:** Practiced venture finance at Fenwick & West (Silicon Valley), antitrust at Cadwalader (DC). J.D. with honors from U. Chicago Law School, B.A. magna cum laude from U. Florida.
