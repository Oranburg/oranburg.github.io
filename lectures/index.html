Trade Secrets

Trade secret law protects confidential business information that derives economic value from its secrecy. This section covers the foundations of the Defend Trade Secrets Act (DTSA), the Uniform Trade Secrets Act (UTSA), reasonable measures to maintain secrecy, and the intersection of trade secrets with employment law, digital assets, and innovation policy.

Professor Oranburg's forthcoming casebook Protecting Trade Secrets (Carolina Academic Press, 2026) forms the backbone of these materials.

Key Topics

  • Definition and elements of a trade secret
  • Reasonable measures and the "reasonable efforts" standard
  • Misappropriation: acquisition, disclosure, and use
  • Employee mobility and non-disclosure agreements
  • Trade secrets vs. patents: strategic choices
  • Remedies: injunctive relief, damages, and exemplary damages

Contracts

Contract law governs when and how the law should enforce voluntary agreements. This section covers offer and acceptance, consideration, defenses to formation, the statute of frauds, performance and breach, and remedies—drawing on Contract Law: Rules, Cases, and Problems (2d ed., Carolina Academic Press, 2025).

Key Topics

  • Offer, acceptance, and the objective theory of contracts
  • Consideration and promissory estoppel
  • Defenses: mistake, fraud, duress, and unconscionability
  • Interpretation and the parol evidence rule
  • Conditions, performance, and breach
  • Expectation, reliance, and restitution damages
  • Smart contracts and automated enforcement

Business Associations

Business associations law covers the organization, governance, and liability of business entities—from sole proprietorships and partnerships to LLCs and corporations. These materials accompany the forthcoming Business Associations: Law of the Firm (Aspen Press, 2027) and the Questions & Answers: Business Associations study guide (Carolina Academic Press, 2024).

Key Topics

  • Agency: actual, apparent, and inherent authority
  • Partnership formation and fiduciary duties
  • LLC operating agreements and member liability
  • Corporate formation: charters, bylaws, and capitalization
  • Duty of care and the business judgment rule
  • Duty of loyalty and conflict of interest transactions
  • Shareholder rights and derivative suits
  • Mergers, acquisitions, and fundamental changes
