Now

What I'm working on this month. This page updates when the work changes (a /now page in the Derek Sivers tradition). Last updated {{ site.time | date: "%B %Y" }}.

Writing

  • Business Associations: Law in Theory and Practice (Aspen Press, forthcoming 2027). Converting the manuscript to DOCX and working through the pre-submission polish. Companion site at oranburg.law/BA.
  • Legitimate University Governance (Dartmouth Law Review, forthcoming). Working through the Word accept/reject pass on the editorial revision before returning to the journal.
  • An Informed, Independent Bar (Mercer Law Review, forthcoming). Drafting the full manuscript toward a late-August submission deadline. The article argues that the bar-licensing pipeline selectively chills protected speech and that Speiser v. Randall's burden-shifting doctrine reaches it.
  • Architects of the Void. Completing physical-source verifications on a law-review essay on the entrepreneurial lawyer as sub-creator in the Catholic intellectual tradition, then submitting.

Teaching

  • Preparing courses for the August start at Columbus School of Law, Catholic University of America. The Law and Entrepreneurship Program's 2026-27 year theme is Entrepreneurship: Curing Poverty, and the curriculum design is running in parallel with the course prep.

Programs and organizing

  • Designing the Law and New Things symposium with Robert T. Miller (Scalia Law, GMU). The symposium is scheduled for fall 2026 at CUA Columbus Law and will publish in the Catholic University Law Review. The panel architecture and participant list are the current decisions.
  • Drafting the Templeton application against a July 15 deadline. The proposed research connects a decade of iterative entrepreneurship pedagogy to a five-phase study in Athens, Jerusalem, and Rome.

Public engagement

  • Serving on the New Hampshire Commission to Study Stable Tokens.
  • Two short essays on artificial intelligence, Jewish text, and Catholic social thought are out with editors. Both turn on the same question: what does older doctrine about responsibility for created things say about who answers for what an AI does.

Side projects

  • The Knight RUP restoration: a crowdsourced cleanup of Frank Knight's Risk, Uncertainty and Profit (1921), aiming to produce the canonical free digital edition.
  • Companion sites for Contract Law, Business Associations, and Judgment Proof.

Reading and thinking

Maimonides on the two purposes of law and the highest form of charity. Pope Leo XIV's Magnifica Humanitas. Aristotle on practical wisdom and the conditions under which a craftsman's judgment becomes a habit of excellence. The legal literature on entrepreneurship as vocation, and what it would mean for a law school's curriculum to take that seriously.
